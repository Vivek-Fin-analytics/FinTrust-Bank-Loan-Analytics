USE fintrust_db;

-- ──  Education vs Risk Analysis ────────
SELECT
    e.EDUCATION                            AS education_level,
    COUNT(*)                               AS total_customers,
    ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / 51336, 1)    AS pct_portfolio,
    ROUND(AVG(e.Credit_Score), 0)          AS avg_credit_score,
    ROUND(AVG(e.NETMONTHLYINCOME), 0)      AS avg_income,
    SUM(CASE WHEN e.Approved_Flag = 'P4'
        THEN 1 ELSE 0 END)                 AS high_risk_count,
    ROUND(SUM(CASE WHEN e.Approved_Flag = 'P4'
        THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) * 100.0
        / COUNT(*), 1)                     AS high_risk_pct,
    ROUND(AVG(i.Tot_Missed_Pmnt), 2)       AS avg_missed_payments
FROM internal_bank_data i
JOIN external_cibil_data e ON i.PROSPECTID = e.PROSPECTID
GROUP BY e.EDUCATION
ORDER BY avg_credit_score DESC;

💡 Why Customer Profile Analysis?
Banks need to understand WHO their risky customers are.
Education level, gender, marital status — all affect
repayment behaviour.
This helps bank target right customers for future loans
and identify which segments need closer monitoring.
RBI also requires demographic analysis in annual reports!
